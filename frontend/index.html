Learn With Flowers

Discover interesting facts about flowers and improve your gardening knowledge.

Flower Anatomy

Flowers typically consist of sepals, petals, stamens, and carpels. The male part, or stamen, consists of the anther and filament. The female part, or carpel, consists of the stigma, style, and ovary.

Pollination

Pollination is the transfer of pollen from the anther to the stigma. This can happen through wind, water, or animals like bees, butterflies, and birds.

Life Cycle

The flower life cycle includes germination, growth, flowering, pollination, and seed dispersal. Some plants are annuals (complete life cycle in one growing season), while others are perennials (live for multiple years).

Watering

Most plants prefer consistent moisture but not soggy conditions. Water at the base of plants to prevent leaf diseases. Morning watering is generally better than evening.

Sunlight

Different plants have different light requirements. Full sun is 6+ hours of direct sunlight, partial sun is 4-6 hours, and shade is less than 4 hours of direct sunlight per day.

Soil & Fertilizer

Most flowering plants prefer well-draining soil rich in organic matter. Fertilize during the growing season with balanced fertilizer or compost for healthy blooms.

Composting

Create your own compost from kitchen scraps and yard waste. It's a natural way to enrich soil and reduces landfill waste.

Water Conservation

Collect rainwater in barrels for garden use. Use drip irrigation or soaker hoses to deliver water directly to plant roots with minimal waste.

Natural Pest Control

Encourage beneficial insects like ladybugs and praying mantises. Use companion planting and organic solutions like neem oil instead of chemical pesticides.
